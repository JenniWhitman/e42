Converting from Hexadecimal to Binary is easy as long as you understand the values of Binary and Hexadecimal.

1. Take the Hexadecimal number and write it out in columns, one digit per column.
2. [This step is only necessary if you have trouble with remembering that A-F = 10-15] Convert the calues to basic decimal.
3. Convert each value to a basic 4 digit binary sequence and sandwich them together. Done!

E 1 2 B Our hex number will be E12B. Enter it across a chart like shown.
14 1 2 11 Because we're usually more comfortable with decimal, I find it helpful to convert to decimal before the binary conversion. In this case, that would be the E and the B. The E becomes a 14 and the B becomes an 11.
1110 0001 0010 1011 Now convert these to binary. These are simple values that we have listed on the Binary Page, but that are also good practice for a quick Decimal to Binary conversion. Either way, these will soon become second nature.
1110 0001 0010 1011 For the final step, we take this long string of ones and zeros and put it together making 1110000100101011 which is the binary equivalent of our hexadecimal number E12B.
