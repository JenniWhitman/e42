Those who know binary and those who don't.

Which are you?

The computer that you're using right now, whether it be a laptop, desktop, Mac, PC, smartphone, or tablet is basically a super smart calculator. These calculators are built on numbers. These numbers are the basis for everything that happens on your computer or device. Even this webpage, in its most primitive form, can be broken down into nothing but ones and zeros. How does this happen? What does it mean? And what are the number systems that run it all? How do they translate from one to another and how are they used?


Binary is the basic way that your computer communicates. It's the basis for everything that it does. Binary is built off a system of 2 states - on and off or 1 and 0. It's a simple system that makes up one bit of computer memory per binary digit. Binary is not the only number system that your computer uses.

Another commonly used system is Hexadecimal. Have you ever had to put a color into a web page for customization? Have you ever gotten a blue screen of death with a memory location? Did you notice a string of letters and numbers together with the digits 0-9 and letters A-F? You probably saw a bit of hexadecimal. This system works off a system of 16 characters (using A-F since we only have 10 digits to work with) that accounts for 8 bits or half a byte on a computer.

Decimal is the number system that most humans learn in pre-school or kindergarten. Decimal consists of 10 digits from 0-9 and is one of the lesser used systems by the computer itself, moreso by the programmer on the other end.
