I know binary.

How about you?

This site is a culmination of a semester's work in science writing. My name is Jenni Whitman and this is my final project for Expo E-42cW at the Harvard Extension school with Tom Akbari. This assignment was finalized for May 1, 2014 and any inquiries can be made through the Contact Page found at the preceding link.


I've been on computers since I was 4. I've broken plenty, fixed plenty, and even learned to program in quite a few languages. Part of programming is learning what's at the heart of that programming - math. 10 years ago, in my start at Northeastern University, I tested out of traditional computer science math courses so they had to place me into engineering calculus. After I spent the semester partying the only two classes that I ended up passing were Calculus and Discrete Mathematics - which I didn't find out until I decided to pursue my education again almost a decade later. In my years of tinkering and learning, I've taken over 40 credit hours of computer science coursework including number theory and statistics. One thing that every budding computer scientist learns early on is the science and magic of numbers and how they relate between their programs and the computers they're running on. This is a relation of that information for someone who might not be looking to program, but just understand what those 1s and 0s mean.
